Pricing · what we get wrong

What product people get wrong about pricing.

The mythWhat's actually true
“Pricing is the price.”Price is what you charge. Pricing is everything that decides it — value, segment, packaging, meter. The number is the last 5%.
“Get the product right, then price it.”Design the product around the price. Willingness-to-pay decides what you build — and what you cut.
“Our value is what the product does.”Value is the gap between you and their next-best option. That gap is your ceiling — and your roadmap.
“Cost tells us what to charge.”Cost is the floor you stand on, not the price you charge. Cost goes last, not first.
“The big call is seats vs. tokens.”The meter is the last mile — derived from the value, not chosen first.
“Set the price, ship it, done.”Pricing is a product — versioned by geography and segment, re-tested every quarter, owned forever.
Pricing · ask these first

The six questions to ask instead.

The questionWhat it surfaces
1 · Who is it for — and what job does it win?Segment + value — who it's not for
2 · What will they pay more to get more of?The value metric → your meter
3 · What would they do if we didn't exist?Willingness-to-pay ceiling — and your roadmap
4 · What's in each box — and why upgrade?Packaging
5 · What's our floor — any tier underwater at scale?Cost / margin — the AI margin time bomb
6 · How will we know in 90 days the price is wrong?Pricing as a living product, not a launch task

Notice what's missing — “seats or tokens?” That's not a question you ask. It's an answer that falls out of the other six.
